Here’s an exhaustive list of common patient medications you may encounter in the field, including brand/generic names, key EMS-relevant details, and red flags:

🫀 Cardiac Medications
1. Beta-Blockers
Atenolol (Tenormin)

Use: HTN, angina

EMS Concern: Masks hypoglycemia/tachycardia; contraindicated in asthma.

Metoprolol (Lopressor, Toprol XL)

Dose: 25-100 mg PO BID

EMS Concern: Avoid in cardiogenic shock.

Propranolol (Inderal)

Use: HTN, migraine

EMS Concern: Can cause severe bradycardia.

2. ACE Inhibitors
Lisinopril (Prinivil, Zestril)

EMS Concern: Angioedema risk (hold NTG).

Enalapril (Vasotec)

Dose: 2.5-40 mg PO daily

Interaction: Hyperkalemia with potassium-sparing diuretics.

3. ARBs
Losartan (Cozaar)

Use: HTN (alternative to ACE inhibitors)

EMS Concern: Less angioedema risk than ACE-Is.

4. Calcium Channel Blockers
Amlodipine (Norvasc)

EMS Concern: Refractory hypotension (treat with calcium gluconate).

Diltiazem (Cardizem)

Use: Afib/HTN

EMS Concern: Bradycardia risk.

5. Antiarrhythmics
Amiodarone (Pacerone)

EMS Concern: Pulmonary toxicity (long-term use).

Digoxin (Lanoxin)

Dose: 0.125-0.25 mg PO daily

Toxicity: Nausea, vision changes, hyperkalemia.

💊 Analgesics & Anti-Inflammatories
6. NSAIDs
Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin)

EMS Concern: GI bleed risk, kidney injury.

Naproxen (Aleve)

Dose: 220-550 mg PO BID

Celecoxib (Celebrex)

Use: Arthritis (less GI bleed risk).

7. Acetaminophen (Tylenol)
Toxicity: >4 g/day → liver failure (antidote: NAC).

8. Opioids
Oxycodone (OxyContin, Percocet)

EMS Concern: Respiratory depression (naloxone reversal).

Hydrocodone (Vicodin)

Dose: 5-10 mg PO q4-6h

Tramadol (Ultram)

Interaction: Seizure risk with SSRIs.

🩸 Blood Thinners
9. Anticoagulants
Warfarin (Coumadin)

EMS Concern: Check INR; bleeding risk (reverse with vitamin K).

Apixaban (Eliquis)

Use: Afib/DVT

Reversal: Andexanet alfa (expensive!).

Clopidogrel (Plavix)

Use: Post-stent

EMS Concern: Bleeding (no reversal agent).

10. Antiplatelets
Aspirin (Bayer, Ecotrin)

Dose: 81-325 mg PO daily

EMS Action: Give 324 mg for ACS.

🌡️ Diabetes Medications
11. Insulins
Glargine (Lantus)

Use: Long-acting basal insulin.

Lispro (Humalog)

Use: Rapid-acting (watch for hypoglycemia).

12. Oral Hypoglycemics
Metformin (Glucophage)

EMS Concern: Lactic acidosis (hold in shock).

Glipizide (Glucotrol)

Risk: Hypoglycemia (especially in elderly).

🧠 Psych & Neuro Meds
13. SSRIs/SNRIs
Sertraline (Zoloft)

Interaction: Serotonin syndrome with tramadol/MAOIs.

Venlafaxine (Effexor)

EMS Concern: Hypertension in overdose.

14. Benzodiazepines
Alprazolam (Xanax)

Dose: 0.25-2 mg PO TID

EMS Concern: Respiratory depression (flumazenil reversal).

15. Antipsychotics
Quetiapine (Seroquel)

Use: Bipolar/schizophrenia

EMS Concern: QT prolongation.

Beta-blocker	Insulin	🍬 Check glucose frequently

Here’s an expanded list including common herbal supplements and their critical interactions with medications, formatted for quick field reference:

🌿 Herbal Supplement Interactions Cheat Sheet
Key: ⚠️ = Dangerous Interaction | 🚫 = Contraindicated | 🔄 = Alters Drug Effects

1. St. John’s Wort (Hypericum perforatum)
Uses: Depression, anxiety

Interactions:
⚠️ SSRIs (e.g., Zoloft): Serotonin syndrome (agitation, hyperthermia)
🔄 Warfarin: Reduces anticoagulant effect → clotting risk
🔄 Digoxin: Lowers blood levels → reduces efficacy
🚫 HIV meds (e.g., indinavir): Treatment failure

2. Ginkgo Biloba
Uses: Memory enhancement

Interactions:
⚠️ Aspirin/Warfarin: Increased bleeding risk (inhibits platelets)
⚠️ NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen): GI bleed synergy
🔄 Antiseizure meds (e.g., Dilantin): Alters metabolism

3. Garlic (Allium sativum)
Uses: Hypertension, cholesterol

Interactions:
⚠️ Warfarin/Plavix: Major bleeding risk
🔄 Saquinavir (HIV): Reduces drug levels by 50%

4. Ginseng (Panax ginseng)
Uses: Energy booster

Interactions:
⚠️ Warfarin: Decreases INR → clotting risk
🔄 Insulin/Oral hypoglycemics: Unpredictable glucose changes

5. Echinacea
Uses: Immune support

Interactions:
🚫 Immunosuppressants (e.g., prednisone): Counteracts effects
🔄 Caffeine: May increase jitteriness

6. Valerian Root
Uses: Insomnia, anxiety

Interactions:
⚠️ Benzodiazepines (e.g., Xanax): Sedation synergy → respiratory depression
🔄 Alcohol: Enhanced CNS depression

7. Licorice Root (Glycyrrhiza glabra)
Uses: Sore throat, ulcers

Interactions:
⚠️ Digoxin: Toxicity risk (hypokalemia)
⚠️ Diuretics (e.g., Lasix): Severe hypokalemia → arrhythmias

8. Kava Kava
Uses: Anxiety relief

Interactions:
🚫 Liver-toxic meds (e.g., statins, acetaminophen): Hepatotoxicity risk
⚠️ Alcohol: Potentiates sedation

9. Saw Palmetto
Uses: BPH symptoms

Interactions:
🔄 Warfarin: Increased bleeding (theoretical)
🔄 Finasteride: May interfere with PSA tests

10. Milk Thistle (Silybum marianum)
Uses: Liver detox

Interactions:
🔄 Antipsychotics (e.g., Haldol): Alters liver metabolism
🔄 Metformin: May enhance hypoglycemia

🚨 High-Risk Herb-Drug Combos
Herb	Avoid With	EMS Concern
St. John’s Wort	SSRIs, Warfarin	Serotonin syndrome, clotting
Ginkgo	Aspirin, NSAIDs	Uncontrolled bleeding
Kava	Benzodiazepines, Alcohol	Respiratory arrest
Licorice	Digoxin, Diuretics	Deadly hypokalemia
