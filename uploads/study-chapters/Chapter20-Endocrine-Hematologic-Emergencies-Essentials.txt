# Chapter 20: Endocrine and Hematologic Emergencies Essentials

## Introduction to Endocrine and Hematologic Emergencies

Endocrine and hematologic emergencies involve disruptions in hormone regulation and blood component disorders. These conditions require rapid recognition and appropriate EMT-B interventions.

### Key Components of Endocrine Assessment:
- **Blood glucose monitoring**: Using glucometer when available
- **Mental status evaluation**: Altered consciousness indicators
- **Vital signs assessment**: Temperature, blood pressure changes
- **Medication history**: Insulin, oral hypoglycemics, steroids

### EMT-B Endocrine Scope:
- Blood glucose testing (where protocols allow)
- Oral glucose administration for conscious hypoglycemic patients
- Patient positioning and airway management
- Rapid transport for definitive treatment
- Documentation of findings and interventions

### Common Endocrine Emergencies:
- **Hypoglycemia**: Blood glucose < 70 mg/dL, altered mental status
- **Hyperglycemia**: Blood glucose > 250 mg/dL, dehydration, altered mental status
- **Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)**: Severe hyperglycemia with ketones
- **Hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state**: Extreme hyperglycemia without ketones

### Hypoglycemia Management:
- **Conscious patient**: Oral glucose 15-20 grams
- **Unconscious patient**: Maintain airway, rapid transport
- **Signs**: Sweating, shakiness, confusion, hunger
- **Contraindications**: Unconscious patient, swallowing difficulties

### Hematologic Emergency Components:
- **Anemia**: Reduced red blood cell count or hemoglobin
- **Bleeding disorders**: Abnormal clotting function
- **Sickle cell crisis**: Painful episodes in sickle cell disease
- **Thrombosis**: Blood clot formation complications

### Blood Glucose Normal Values:
- Normal fasting: 70-100 mg/dL
- Normal random: 70-140 mg/dL
- Hypoglycemic: < 70 mg/dL
- Hyperglycemic: > 180 mg/dL (postprandial)

### Recognition Signs:
- **Hypoglycemic**: Diaphoresis, tachycardia, confusion, hunger
- **Hyperglycemic**: Polyuria, polydipsia, fruity breath odor, dehydration
- **Hematologic**: Pallor, weakness, abnormal bleeding, pain

Endocrine and hematologic emergency management focuses on rapid assessment, glucose correction when appropriate, and expedited transport to advanced care facilities.
